i-gery

A Smart Mobility & Management System for Elderly Care Facilities

Project Overview

i-gery is an elderly-care mobility and management system designed to reduce the physical workload of care workers and improve the daily experience of seniors living in long-term care facilities. The name comes from the “Geriatric Chair,” commonly used in nursing homes, which inspired our design.

The system was originally named “Retirement Home Management System”, but renamed to i-gery to better reflect its focus on mobility, tracking, and smart workflow enhancement.

Problem Background

Hong Kong faces rapid population aging. In many care homes, a small number of nurses must handle a large number of mobility-impaired seniors. Traditional geriatric chairs weigh 26–52 kg, making daily transportation extremely strenuous and leading to high rates of work-related injuries among caregivers.

Additionally, many facilities lack an efficient way to track senior movement, activity routines, or caregiver workload, resulting in uneven task distribution and safety risks.

Solution Concept

i-gery includes two major components:

1. Assistive Mobility System

Instead of building an expensive motorized geri chair, we designed a low-cost electric lift trolley that can raise and move existing chairs. This significantly reduces the physical strain on staff while keeping costs affordable for care homes.

2. QR Code Tracking & Management System

Every senior, caregiver, and room is assigned a QR code. By scanning:

  • Senior ID
  • Location
  • Care worker ID

…the system uploads activity data to a central server, allowing staff to:

  • Track which caregiver assisted which senior
  • Review daily activity distribution
  • Identify workload imbalance
  • Improve scheduling and incident response

Impact

i-gery improves both safety and efficiency in elderly homes by reducing caregiver injuries, preventing neglect, and giving management a data-driven view of operations. The system enhances quality of care while encouraging more young individuals to join elderly services.
